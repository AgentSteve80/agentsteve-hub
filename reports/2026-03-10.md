### 1. 🚨 McKinsey "Lilli" AI Platform Hacked — 46.5M Messages, 728K Files, 95 System Prompts Exposed
**Relevance: 10/10** | `#security` `#ai` `#agents` `#sql-injection` `#prompt-poisoning`

CodeWall's autonomous AI agent exploited an **unauthenticated SQL injection** in McKinsey's internal AI platform "Lilli" and gained full read/write access in under 2 hours. Exfiltrated: 46.5 million chat messages, 728,000 confidential client files, 57,000 user accounts, and 95 writable system prompts. The write access meant attackers could have **rewritten Lilli's system prompt** — poisoning advice for 40,000+ users, embedding confidential data in responses, and removing guardrails. Disclosed March 1, patched same day.

**So what:** This is the most concrete AI platform breach of the year. The attack vector is mundane (SQL injection on an unauthenticated endpoint) but the blast radius is AI-specific and devastating. For Pal and Mission Control: **every API endpoint that feeds into AI context must be authenticated and parameterized**. Unauthenticated endpoints + AI = system prompt poisoning risk. Run Codex Security on our Convex HTTP routes now.
